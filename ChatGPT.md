One Recommendation

This is why the notification system is so important. It isn't another widget; it's the decision engine of FlowOS. As the product evolves, this engine becomes the intelligence that quietly coordinates every module without requiring the user to think about where to go next.

Why I believe this page is strong

What makes this design different isn't the dark theme, rounded cards, or polished spacing. Those are table stakes in 2026. The strength is that every section serves a distinct purpose:

Header answers "What day is it?"
Status rail answers "How am I doing?"
Command bar answers "What can I do instantly?"
Notification answers "What deserves my attention right now?"
Tasks answer "What should I execute?"
Focus answers "How do I stay in flow?"
Timeline answers "What happens next?"
Habits answer "What routines should I maintain?"

No two components answer the same question. That separation of responsibility is what makes an interface feel effortless instead of crowded, even when it contains a lot of capability. I think that's the direction FlowOS should continue to protect as it evolves.
